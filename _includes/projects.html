Mitigating Gender Bias in Word Embeddings

Challenge: Recognizing that pretrained word embeddings often carry inherent gender biases, which can perpetuate unfairness in downstream applications, I aimed to address this critical issue.

Approach: I implemented an Adversarial apporach to make the embeddings resistant to bias-inducing perturbations.

Outcome: These interventions led to a 68% reduction in gender bias within GloVe embeddings, promoting fairness in tasks like resume screening and candidate selection.

Word Sense Disambiguation in Biomedical Text

Challenge: Biomedical literature is rife with ambiguous terms, making accurate information retrieval and analysis challenging.

Approach: I applied Markov clustering algorithms to the PubMed dataset, effectively grouping contextually similar terms.

Outcome: This method achieved 85% accuracy in resolving ambiguities, significantly enhancing the precision of text-mining applications in biomedical research.

Image Super-Resolution

Challenge: Enhancing image resolution without compromising quality is vital for applications ranging from medical imaging to security.

Approach: I combined Super-Resolution Generative Adversarial Networks (SRGAN) with self-supervised learning techniques to upscale images by 400%.

Challenge: Accurately interpreting human emotions through facial expressions is essential for applications like human-computer interaction and security.

Approach: I trained Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) on the FER2013 dataset to classify seven distinct emotions.

Outcome: The model achieved 75% accuracy, and I developed a real-time demo that processes live camera input for immediate emotion recognition.
